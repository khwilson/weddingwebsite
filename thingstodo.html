Kentucky is a great place to visit for all sorts of reasons. Depending on how long you want to stay in or around Louisville, there are several excursions to take. Here are a few suggestions.

Bourbon

Kentucky is the home of bourbon and distilleries love to show guests around. We recommend you go to a few to compare. The distilleries are generally clumped around Lousiville, Bardstown, and Versailles (pronounced ver-SAYLZ; you're not in France!) What follows are some distilleries and "bourbon experiences" in each region.

The Bardstown and Versailles clusters require driving. One option is to rent a limo for a day, which can run between $50 and $100 per person depending on how fancy you want to be and how many people are going. If you would like to sign up for a limo with other wedding guests, please fill out this survey and the grooms will coordinate.

Louisville

There are several micro-distilleries and "bourbon experiences" very close to Downtown Louisville. Ride hailing will probably be sufficient to get around.

  • Angel's Envy Distillery: Located on Louisville’s historic “Whiskey Row." Tours run every 30 minutes on Fridays and weekends and last about an hour. $15 per person. Tickets must be purchased in advance.
  • Evan Williams Experience: Also located on Louisville’s historic “Whiskey Row." Tours run every 30 minutes on Fridays and weekends and last about an hour. $12 for adults, $9 for kids aged 11-20, and free for kids under 10.
  • Jim Beam Microdistillery: This is a smaller downtown outpost (but still includes a working distillery) of the larger operation about 30 minutes outside Louisville. Open on Friday and Saturday, but closed Sunday. No reservations necessary. Tastings start at $5 for four bourbons.
  • Bulleit Experience: About 15 minutes from downtown by car at a working distillery. Tours available all weekend without a reservation. $10 for 21 and over; free for under 21.

Bardstown

Bardstown is about 45 minutes from Downtown Louisville and the distilleries are a bit farther apart from each other. On the other hand, it is home to more active distilleries than downtown, and you just might prefer their bourbons.

  • Jim Beam Distillery: Open all weekend with tours lasting about 90 minutes. $14 for 21 and over; $7 for ages 10-20.
  • Four Roses Bottling and Warehousing: Open all weekend but reservations are recommended as this is a somewhat smaller facility (but still worth the visit). Tastings are $5.
  • Old Heaven Hill Distillery/Bourbon Heritage Center: Open all weekend. Offers multiple tour and tasting options. Prices start at $10.
  • Willett Distillery: Open all weekend with tours offered on the hour. Reservations recommended. $15; no charge for visitors under 21.

Versailles

Versailles is about an hour from Downtown Louisville and the distilleries are a bit farther apart from each other. Like the options in Bardstown, however, it is home to more active distilleries than downtown, and you just might prefer their bourbons.

  • Buffalo Trace Distillery
  • Four Roses Distillery
  • Wild Turkey Distillery
  • Woodford Reserve Distillery

Horses

Kentucky is also the center of the world's thoroughbred industry. This consists of two major racetracks as well as several major farms. Some of the industry is in Louisville, but much of it is in Lexington, a little over an hour east of downtown. Here are some potential things to see.

Churchill Downs

The home of the Kentucky Derby, Churchill Downs is probably the most famous horse racetrack. On October 27, however, there will be no live racing, but it is open for tours, including the Derby museum. (If you stick around until the next weekend, though, the Breeder's Cup will be racing on the first Friday and Saturday in November.)

Keeneland

The lesser known but probably more important track is Keeneland, located in Lexington. All October racing in Kentucky happens at Keeneland's Fall Meet. From October 5 through October 27, there are live races every day but Monday and Tuesday. Tours are also avaiable if booked ahead of time at Keeneland's website. If you're willing to get up a bit early, the behind-the-scenes racing tour is excellent. Note that Keeneland is (as of early February) finalizing their April through December schedule, and anticipates is being posted by late February.

Horse Farms

There are several horse farms in the Lexington area, including the farm where American Pharoah, the last Triple Crown winner, hangs out and collects stud fees. Unfortunately, we're a little early to book fall tours of horse farms at the moment, but keep them in mind if you're going to Keeneland.

College Basketball

College basketball is a huge part of the Kentucky psyche. The schedules for University of Kentucky and University of Louisville basketball isn't out yet. However, the last Friday of October typically has UofL and UK playing exhibition matches. We'll update this space when the schedules are released.

Louisville Museums

Louisville has several great museums that Kevin remembers from his childhood. They are all located within walking or cabbing distance from the Seelbach, and so can make for a relaxing Saturday.

Slugger Museum

One of Louisville's primary exports is Louisville Slugger baseball bats. The factory has a museum attached to it, showcasing many of the bats used by baseball's greatest players.

Kentucky Science Center

Kevin loves science museums. Like all science museums, this one is aimed at kids, but it makes it no less fun.

Speed Museum

The Speed Museum is the largest art museum in the Louisville area, located on the University of Louisville's campus. A free highlights tour occurs on Saturdays at 1pm.

Food

Louisville is home to some great restaurants. They tend to be located in either Downtown Louisville or a couple miles away on Bardstown Road. Here are a few suggestions.

  • 610 Magnolia: Ed Lee's fancy, pricy, prix fixe restaurant. Often described as the best restaurant in Louisville.
  • Milkwood: Ed Lee's less fancy restaurant. The food is great, but the bourbon bar is the real star.
  • Jack Fry's: *The* Louisville restaurant since the 1930s.
  • Mark's Feed Store: A great local barbecue chain.
  • Brown Hotel Bar: Another classic Louisville restaurant that debuted several local staples, including the "Hot Brown." Yes, it is just the hotel bar.
  • Mayan Cafe: A relatively new Pan-Latin restaurant that people are talking about.
  • Hammerheads: Southern staples and burgers. Note that they only do dinner.

Longer stays

If you're sticking around for a little longer, consider some of the outdoor activities in Kentucky. These include Mammoth Cave, Natural Bridge (and the nearby sandstone climbing), and parts of the Appalachian Trail.
